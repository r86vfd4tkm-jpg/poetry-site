Morning, Unhurried

The kettle hums its single note,
steam unspooling like a thought
I haven't finished thinking yet.
Outside, the street is still asleep —
and for a moment, so am I,
content to let the daylight keep.

What the River Knows

It does not argue with the stone.
It simply finds another way,
and carries what it cannot move
a little further every day.
Be patient, then, with your own grief —
water, too, was taught by leaving.

from Tideline

Inventory

One chair, warm from your sitting.
One window, full of borrowed sky.
One cup, the chip you said gave it character.
How rich a quiet room can be
when you decide to notice it.

from Held Light
